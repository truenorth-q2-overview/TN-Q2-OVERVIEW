Lower Elementary · Quarter II 2026

4th Grade

📖 Language Arts

Reading Workshop

This quarter, students will read The One and Only Ivan by Katherine Applegate as a class anchor text. Through this novel, they will deepen their skills in character study, learning to track how characters change over time, infer characters' motivations and feelings from their actions and dialogue, and develop theories about why characters behave the way they do.

Students will also work on making inferences across the text, using evidence to support their thinking. They will practice close reading strategies, paying attention to how authors use descriptive language, symbolism, and point of view to create meaning. Independent reading will continue alongside the class novel, with students applying the same character-analysis skills to their self-selected books.

Writing Workshop

In writing, students will move into research-based writing, learning to craft informational reports that are well-organized, clearly structured, and supported by evidence. They will learn how to gather information from multiple sources, take organized notes, and properly cite their references.

Students will focus on structuring their reports into well-developed chapters or sections, each with a clear focus. They will practice writing strong introductions and conclusions, using transition words to connect ideas, and integrating facts, definitions, and details to support their main points. Revision and editing will be emphasized throughout the process.

🔢 Mathematics

In the second quarter of 4th grade, Units 3 and 4 build on foundational skills with a focus on multiplication, division, multi-digit operations, and measurement.

Unit 3 — Multiplication & Division

Students will extend their multiplication skills to include 2- and 3-digit multiplication, building fluency with multi-digit problems. They will also tackle division, including problems with and without remainders, learning to interpret remainders in real-world contexts. This unit strengthens their understanding of the relationship between multiplication and division and builds the computational fluency needed for more complex problem solving.

Unit 4 — Addition, Subtraction & Measurement

This unit focuses on multi-digit addition and subtraction using standard algorithms, ensuring students can solve problems accurately and efficiently. Students will also explore measurement conversion, learning to convert between units of length, mass, and capacity within the same measurement system. Practical, real-world problems will help students see the relevance of these skills in everyday life.

Together, these units provide a rigorous mathematical experience, combining computational fluency with practical measurement applications.

🎨 Project-Based Learning

“Enlightened Tours In Costa Rica” — This quarter, students will explore the fascinating world of nocturnal animals, animal senses, light and survival, and how night tourism and eco-tourism intersect in Costa Rica. They will investigate how animals have adapted to life in the dark and how humans can responsibly experience these wonders.

Driving Question: How can we design engaging and responsible night tour experiences that showcase Costa Rica’s nocturnal wildlife while promoting conservation and sustainable tourism?

Science Focus: Animal Senses & Light

Students will investigate how nocturnal animals use specialized senses to navigate, hunt, and survive in darkness. They will explore the science of light, how different animals perceive it, and how light pollution affects wildlife behavior and habitats.

Social Studies Focus: Tourism, Environment & Responsibility

Students will examine Costa Rica’s eco-tourism industry, exploring how tourism impacts both the economy and the environment. They will investigate what makes tourism sustainable and responsible, and how communities can balance economic opportunity with environmental protection.

PBL Skills Focus

  • Inquiry: Formulating questions, conducting research, and synthesizing findings about nocturnal ecosystems.
  • Collaboration: Working in teams to plan, design, and present tour experiences.
  • Communication: Presenting research and proposals clearly to authentic audiences.

Assessment

  • Science Models: Students will create models demonstrating how nocturnal animals use their senses and how light affects wildlife behavior.
  • Social Studies Research: Students will research and analyze the impact of tourism on Costa Rica’s ecosystems, presenting their findings with supporting evidence.
  • Integrated Final Products: Students will design night tour stations, create wildlife guides, and develop sustainable tourism proposals that combine their science and social studies learning.

🇨🇷 Español

Lectura: “La mujer que sabía todos los cuentos”

Este bimestre, los estudiantes explorarán este texto que se adentra en la tradición oral, el papel de las mujeres en la preservación de historias y la riqueza de la historia latinoamericana. A través de la lectura, los estudiantes analizarán temas de identidad cultural, roles de género a lo largo del tiempo y la importancia de la narración oral como patrimonio cultural.

Escritura

Los estudiantes se enfocarán en la producción de textos descriptivos, utilizando adjetivos, metáforas y lenguaje sensorial para crear descripciones vivas y detalladas de personajes, escenarios y situaciones. Practicarán la elaboración de párrafos bien estructurados con ideas claras y conectores apropiados.

Gramática

El enfoque gramatical incluirá la concordancia de género y número, el uso correcto de adjetivos calificativos, adverbios y pronombres. Los estudiantes aplicarán estas reglas gramaticales tanto en sus escritos como en su comunicación oral.

Ortografía

Los estudiantes trabajarán en el uso correcto de la h, la g y la j, sílabas complejas y reglas de acentuación. Mediante ejercicios prácticos y dictados, reforzarán su precisión ortográfica.

Producción Oral

Los estudiantes desarrollarán habilidades de expresión oral a través de descripciones orales de personajes y escenarios. Practicarán la presentación clara y fluida de sus ideas, utilizando vocabulario descriptivo y estructuras gramaticales apropiadas para comunicar sus observaciones y análisis.
