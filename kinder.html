“The Explorers”

Welcome to the "Explorers" group at Let's Play by True North. The Explorers are our curious 4-year-olds, turning 5 during the year. This is a time for deeper exploration, creativity, and collaboration, as they begin to engage in more complex play and learning. The Explorers are eager to chart new paths, expand their skills, and build confidence in their growing independence. Together, we'll navigate an exciting year of discovery and growth!

How Do We Learn Throughout the Year?

In the "Explorers" program at True North, children learn through purposeful and dynamic play. At this stage, their learning expands to include more structured activities that encourage critical thinking, creativity and collaboration. While maintaining autonomy, children engage in projects and explorations that deepen their understanding of the world around them. Our environments are designed to spark curiosity and promote foundational skills in all areas of development.

Through guided interactions and intentional teaching moments, Explorers develop problem-solving abilities, teamwork, and communication skills. This balanced approach ensures they build confidence and independence while forming strong social and emotional connections that support their growth.

How and When Are Parents Informed About Growth?

  • Developmental Milestones Report Card (DMR): Based on the True North curriculum for early ages and developmental expectations, teachers will identify and collect evidence of growth and report it in the Developmental Milestones Report (DMR). This growth is observed in daily interactions, exploration time and playful experiences. Sent home at the end of every quarter via e-mail.
  • Student Portfolio: Twice a year parents will receive a digital portfolio with pictures and videos of each child reflecting their growth along overall standards.
  • Parent-Teacher Conferences: Concluding each semester, teachers will meet with all families.
  • Show My Learning: A presentation designed to provide an opportunity to connect school and family and showcase how children experience preschool life.

Awaken Your True North Program

Our Awaken Your True North program positions the development of a series of essential skills for life and leadership (core skills) as the backbone of our educational model, transforming the school experience into an awakening of conscience and therefore contributing to students' well-being and success in their lives as agents of change in society.

Our core skills are: self-care, appreciation, audacity, commitment, curiosity, empathy, and neutrality. Throughout the day our program enriches students' day with different practices.

Areas of Development

💜 Social & Emotional Learning

Social-emotional development serves as the foundation for much of a child's learning. For this reason, it is highly valued in our Early Childhood program. Guiding children to develop skills that foster strong relationships, enhance emotional awareness, and enable them to recognize, understand, and appropriately express their feelings is of utmost importance.

Using the Conscious Discipline approach, we support toddlers and preschoolers in exploring their emotions, using their "Big Voice" to assert themselves and resolve social conflicts, and learning strategies to maintain composure. Furthermore, they will grow in the understanding of other's perspectives and feelings, developing empathy and considering others' ideas and feelings when socializing.

During this year, they will learn about the uniqueness each member of their class has and how make each of them special and valued.

🌱 Independence

Developing independence in our students lets them know that we value them so that they grow up with a strong sense of self and belief that they can do anything they put their mind to. Not only does this help our children grow and develop, but it also fosters them to be confident and competent communicators, curious and resilient explorers, and creative thinkers.

Kinder Explorers develop greater independence through a consistent routine. When children can anticipate their day, they are better equipped to take on responsibilities. This year we support Kinder Explorers to develop independence by having choices — teachers provide two or three options, by offering a choice they can feel empowered and valued. Kinder Explorers will also learn how to take care of themselves and their belongings.

🧠 Cognitive Skills

In Kinder, students will continue to strengthen problem-solving skills and the capacity to understand the world around them and figure out things by themselves, using their five senses. By bringing real-life context to the curriculum through a Project Based Learning (PBL) approach, students are encouraged to become critical thinkers, and lifelong learners.

Students learn to take responsibility for their own learning, forming the basis for the way they will work with others, providing opportunities for them to collaborate, learn about self-management, investigate, ask questions and acquire knowledge. Children are developing skills such as:

  • Attention and response
  • Memory
  • Information processing
  • Problem-solving
  • Understanding cause and effect
  • Pattern recognition
  • Representation

💬 Language

Kinder Explorers thrive in an English-speaking environment enriched with playful, meaningful learning experiences. They build language skills by using common words, commands, and complete sentences, progressing in their understanding and use of English grammar, including nouns, verbs, adjectives, and prepositions. Teachers support language acquisition with visuals, gestures, simplified speech, and mirroring.

Reading is a key part of the daily routine, fostering curiosity, imagination, and role-play while helping students connect personally with stories, retell events, and make predictions. Early reading activities introduce children to the conventions of literacy and storytelling.

Students engage with the Letterland program to explore letter sounds through multisensory activities like music, storytelling, and dramatic play. They also participate in the Handwriting Without Tears program, which uses hands-on activities, such as forming shapes and letters with manipulatives, tracing, and drawing, to develop writing readiness and fine motor skills.

🔢 Math

Our math curriculum enhances student's ability to find multiple solutions, promoting critical thinking and creativity through many dynamic visual models. Also, through age-appropriate play experiences, exploration of the world and guided conversations, the Explorers are invited to see math and learn the vocabulary to describe what they're noticing around them related to how math is in our daily lives.

They will be developing the ability to understand numbers and the relationships between them through dynamic activities, the use of concrete materials and fun stories. Along the school year the Explorers will learn about:

  • How each number is represented by its own symbol and quantity
  • How to solve real world mathematical problems and operations
  • The relationship between numbers
  • How numbers can be composed and decomposed (put together and taken apart in different ways)
