5th Grade

📖 Language Arts

Reading Workshop

Tackling Complexity: Moving up Levels of Nonfiction

In this nonfiction unit, students will immerse themselves in a novel that offers a window into real-life situations, fostering empathy for the characters. This exploration encourages an appreciation for the beauty found in both cultural similarities and differences. Understanding the plot’s development hinges on considering the context and surroundings, prompting students to engage in personal research on historical matters.

Together, the class will delve into A Long Walk to Water by Linda Sue Park, a poignant tale set in war-torn Sudan. The narrative follows eleven-year-old Salva’s harrowing journey as a refugee, paralleled by the daily struggles of eleven-year-old Nya, who walks miles to fetch water for her family. Both children exhibit resilience against unimaginable odds, their stories converging in a narrative of hope.

Writing Workshop

The Lens of History: Research Reports

This unit challenges students to craft informative essays and texts. Using their class novel as a model of nonfiction text, students will deconstruct the context and backstory, deepen their comprehension by researching topics from the novel, and refine their writing skills to eloquently convey their understanding of the material. This integrated approach enhances their ability to critically analyze and articulate historical narratives.

🔢 Mathematics

In the second quarter of 5th grade, Units 3 and 4 focus on enhancing students’ understanding of decimals and advancing their multiplication and division skills.

Unit 3 — Place Value & Decimals

This unit teaches students to read, write, order, and compare decimal numbers. They practice adding and subtracting decimals to the hundredths place, identify equivalent fractions and decimals, and multiply and divide whole and decimal numbers by powers of ten. The unit also emphasizes practical applications, such as solving money problems and using models to represent decimal place value.

Unit 4 — Multiplying & Dividing Whole Numbers & Decimals

This unit builds computational fluency with a focus on multi-digit multiplication and division. Students learn a variety of strategies for solving these problems, including the standard algorithm, ratio tables, and partial products. They also begin multiplying and dividing decimal numbers, connecting these skills to real-world contexts such as calculating costs and quantities. Throughout the unit, students refine their understanding of place value and explore the relationship between multiplication and division.

These units work together to strengthen foundational skills while introducing more complex concepts and problem-solving strategies, preparing them for success in future mathematical challenges.

🎨 Project-Based Learning

Earth’s Systems — The Performance Expectations in Earth’s Systems help students formulate an answer to the question, “How do Earth’s systems interact and shape the planet?”

Fifth-grade students explore the interactions between the geosphere, hydrosphere, atmosphere, and biosphere, learning how these systems affect each other through natural processes like the water cycle and erosion. Students investigate Earth’s water distribution and create models to simulate how water moves through its various states and locations on the planet.

They also study natural disasters, such as hurricanes and earthquakes, to understand how Earth’s systems interact during extreme events. By the end of their studies, students understand the dynamic nature of Earth and how humans impact its systems.

Crosscutting Concepts

  • Systems and systems models: Understanding how Earth’s subsystems interact as a complex whole.
  • Scale and proportion: Examining processes at different scales, from local erosion to global water cycles.
  • Cause and effect: Analyzing how changes in one system trigger responses in others.

🇨🇷 Español

Lectura

Este bimestre se centrará en la lectura de Cuando callaron las armas de Etna Iturralde, una novela que presenta las experiencias de niños que viven en contextos de guerra. A través de la lectura, los estudiantes analizarán cómo los autores describen ambientes, emociones y situaciones, y cómo estos elementos ayudan a comprender el impacto de los conflictos en la vida de las personas. Se fomentará la empatía, el pensamiento crítico y la conexión entre los textos y la realidad.

Escritura

Los estudiantes desarrollarán textos descriptivos en los que aprenderán a representar personas, lugares, objetos y emociones mediante el uso de detalles específicos y lenguaje sensorial. Se trabajará la organización lógica de las ideas, el uso de conectores básicos y la ampliación del vocabulario descriptivo para evitar repeticiones. A través de la planificación, la redacción y la revisión, los estudiantes fortalecerán su capacidad para crear descripciones claras, ricas y visuales.

Gramática

El trabajo gramatical se enfocará en el uso de pronombres, adjetivos, adverbios y preposiciones para lograr descripciones más precisas y cohesionadas. Estos elementos permitirán que los estudiantes conecten ideas, expresen relaciones espaciales y temporales, y enriquezcan el lenguaje de sus textos descriptivos. La gramática será aplicada de manera constante dentro de las producciones escritas y orales.

Ortografía

Se reforzará el uso de la acentuación en palabras agudas, graves y esdrújulas, así como en palabras complejas. Los estudiantes aplicarán estas reglas en sus textos descriptivos y en ejercicios de revisión, mejorando la precisión y la calidad de su escritura.

Producción Oral

Los estudiantes realizarán descripciones orales de personajes, ambientes y situaciones presentes en la novela. Estas actividades fortalecerán su capacidad para expresarse con claridad, enriquecer su vocabulario y organizar ideas al hablar, conectando sus interpretaciones personales con los temas centrales del texto.
