Subjects This Quarter

📖 Language Arts

In the latter part of the semester, students will persist in crafting narratives and progressing towards incorporating more written words into their papers. With ongoing support, including dictating stories to adults, they will successfully write and share their narratives with peers. As this unit concludes, students will transition to creating pattern books. Following the gradual release model, they will initially receive a specific pattern to write, later selecting sentence starters. By the unit's end, they will confidently produce their own pattern books with increased independence.

Oral language production remains a focus to enhance their vocabulary. Phonics instruction remains integral for writing, allowing students to apply letter sounds and proper letter formation in real-life settings.

Throughout this quarter, the emphasis will persist on mastering the alphabet. Additionally, students will concentrate on key phonological awareness skills such as matching medial sounds, blending and segmenting 3-sound words with digraphs, adding initial phonemes, and deleting initial phonemes.

🔢 Mathematics

In the second quarter of Prep, Units 3 and 4 build upon the foundational math skills established earlier, focusing on advanced number concepts and arithmetic. Unit 3 introduces children to counting by 2s up to 20, helping them recognize patterns in even numbers and understand the concept of doubles. This unit emphasizes pattern recognition and sets a foundation for more advanced number concepts.

Unit 4 delves into counting both forwards and backwards from 0 to 50, reinforcing number sequencing skills. It also introduces children to ordering and comparing numbers up to 10, and lays the groundwork for basic arithmetic by teaching addition and subtraction within 10. Furthermore, Unit 4 incorporates practical applications of math, such as comparing object lengths and using pennies and nickels for understanding value and basic financial concepts.

Collectively, these units enhance number sense, arithmetic skills, measurement understanding, and financial literacy, all presented through engaging and age-appropriate activities designed for young learners.

🎨 Project-Based Learning

“Senses In The Dark” — This quarter, Prep students will embark on an exciting and playful PBL journey. Through captivating stories, and imaginative design challenges, they will uncover the mysteries of nocturnal creatures that visit our school. Students will explore what these animals need to thrive, and how we can help protect them.

As part of their learning, students will write pattern books to share what they've discovered about night animals and their habitats. They will also design and build playful, animal-friendly structures, such as shadow shelters or mini habitats, to provide safe spaces for these creatures.

This engaging unit ignites curiosity and nurtures empathy, encouraging students to see themselves as stewards of the natural world. This project inspires critical thinking about human impact on the environment and fosters a lifelong connection to nature.
