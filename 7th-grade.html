📖 Language Arts

Reading Workshop

In this unit, students will engage with contemporary literature through Inside Out & Back Again by Thanhhà Lai, a verse novel that tells the story of Hà, a young girl forced to flee Vietnam during the war and begin a new life in the United States. Told in free-verse poems, the novel captures Hà’s experiences of loss, cultural displacement, and resilience as she navigates the challenges of immigration and prejudice.

Students will focus on analyzing how the author uses poetic language, structure, and imagery to express Hà’s emotions and to convey both personal and historical experiences. The unit will highlight themes of identity, family, belonging, and resilience, giving students an opportunity to reflect on how poetry can be both a personal expression and a window into cultural history. By examining these elements, students will strengthen their ability to interpret literature written in verse and develop a deeper appreciation of how storytelling takes shape through poetry.

Writing Workshop

In this writing unit, students will focus on developing their skills as poets by creating a Thematic Poetry Portfolio. Building on the themes explored in Inside Out & Back Again, students will write a series of original free-verse poems that reflect on ideas such as home, language, family, change, belonging, and resilience.

Each week, students will draft, revise, and polish a poem that explores one of these themes, experimenting with figurative language, imagery, tone, and structure. Alongside their poems, students will write short reflections explaining their creative choices, and conclude the unit with an author’s preface that connects their work to the broader themes of the unit. By the end of the unit, students will produce a polished portfolio of poetry that demonstrates both their growth as writers and their ability to use poetry as a medium for voice and expression.

🔢 Mathematics

Module 2: Algebra and Proportionality

In this module, students analyze proportional relationships and expand their algebraic thinking. Students represent proportional relationships using tables, graphs, and equations, and apply algebraic reasoning to expressions, equations, and inequalities. Real-world contexts support understanding and problem solving throughout the module.

What Students Will Do

  • Identify and represent proportional relationships
  • Use unit rates to solve problems
  • Write, simplify, and factor expressions
  • Solve two-step equations
  • Represent and interpret inequalities on a number line

Topic 1: Proportional Relationships

Students use unit rates, tables, graphs, and equations to analyze proportional relationships. Emphasis is placed on understanding constant rate of change and interpreting graphs in context. (7.RP.1, 7.RP.2)

Topic 2: Two-Step Expressions and Equations

Students expand and factor expressions and solve two-step equations. They apply properties of operations and explain how algebraic expressions represent real-world situations. (7.EE.1, 7.EE.2, 7.EE.3, 7.EE.4A)

Topic 3: Inequalities

Students solve and graph inequalities and interpret solutions in context. Visual models and number lines help students understand solution sets. (7.EE.4B)

🔬 Science

Weather and Climate

The performance expectations in Weather and Climate help students formulate an answer to the question: “What factors interact and influence weather and climate?” Three sub-ideas from the NRC Framework are addressed in these performance expectations: ESS2.C, ESS2.D, and ESS3.D.

Students can construct and use models to develop understanding of the factors that control weather and climate. A systems approach is also important here, examining the feedbacks between systems as energy from the sun is transferred between systems and circulates through the ocean and atmosphere.

Crosscutting Concepts

  • Cause and effect: Understanding how energy transfer drives weather patterns and climate systems.
  • Systems and system models: Modeling interactions between the ocean, atmosphere, and sun.
  • Stability and change: Examining how climate systems maintain equilibrium or shift over time.

In the MS Weather and Climate performance expectations, students are expected to demonstrate proficiency in asking questions, developing and using models, and planning and carrying out investigations; and to use these practices to demonstrate understanding of the core ideas.

🇨🇷 Español

En este bimestre, la lectura se orienta a profundizar en la identidad y la sensibilidad a través de una narrativa poética y simbólica. Con Cierto Azul como texto central, los estudiantes fortalecerán el análisis de personajes, emociones y temas, así como la escritura reflexiva y argumentativa. Se integrarán textos no literarios para ampliar perspectivas y sostener interpretaciones con razones y evidencia.

Lectura (obra central)

Se leerá Cierto Azul para analizar el desarrollo del personaje, el conflicto interno y los temas relacionados con identidad, emociones y crecimiento personal. Se trabajará la estructura narrativa y el uso del lenguaje simbólico.

Escritura

Producciones centradas en respuestas interpretativas, análisis de personaje (rasgos, motivaciones y cambios), textos reflexivos breves y un texto argumentativo corto en el que se justifiquen interpretaciones con evidencia.

Lengua y estilo

Profundización en cohesión textual mediante conectores temporales y lógicos; uso de verbos y adjetivos precisos; ampliación de vocabulario por contexto.

Ortografía/convenciones

Acentuación (agudas, graves y esdrújulas), signos de interrogación y exclamación, y corrección de errores frecuentes durante el proceso de revisión.

Textos no literarios integrados

Textos breves sobre identidad, emociones, comunicación interpersonal y expresión artística, así como artículos cortos de opinión para contrastar perspectivas.

Oralidad

Conversaciones guiadas sobre temas de la obra y presentaciones breves explicando la evolución de un personaje o un símbolo.

Evaluación (panorama)

Comprobaciones sumativas de lectura, trabajos de análisis formativos, una tarea escrita extendida (sumativa intermedia) y un producto final centrado en personaje y tema.
