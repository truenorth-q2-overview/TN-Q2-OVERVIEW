Lower Elementary · Quarter II 2026

2nd Grade

📖 Language Arts

Reading Workshop

The main focus of this quarter will be for students to become the kind of readers who learn information about the world from non-fiction books and have big ideas about that reading. Students will learn to use text features, ask questions, and reflect on new learning. They will also learn how to identify the main idea and give details in a text. Furthermore, we will be continuing with our weekly guided reading groups to work on fluency and comprehension.

Writing Workshop

Students will write informative/explanatory texts where they introduce a topic, use facts and definitions, and provide a concluding statement. They will learn to further develop and strengthen their writing by planning, revising, editing, rewriting, or trying a new approach. Students will have the opportunity to strengthen their speaking and listening skills while they present their work to their peers and learn how to participate effectively in a range of conversations with diverse partners.

Phonics

This quarter students will study:

  • Possessive nouns
  • Long and short vowel sounds; open and closed syllable types; V/CV and VC/V syllable division rules
  • Closed syllables with long vowels (-old, -ost, -olt, -ild, -ind)
  • Long a spelling patterns: a_e, ai, ay
  • Long e spelling patterns: e_e, e, ee, ea, -y
  • Review long a and e spelling patterns; "drop the e, add 'ing'" rule
  • Long i spelling patterns: i_e, igh, -y
  • Long o spelling patterns: o_e, oe, -oa, ow, o
  • Long u spelling patterns: u_e, ew, ue, u
  • Final /k/ spelling patterns: -ck, -k, -ke

🔢 Mathematics

In the second quarter of 2nd grade, Units 3 and 4 continue to build on the mathematical skills of students with a focus on addition, subtraction, and measurement.

Unit 3 — Addition & Subtraction Within One Hundred

Emphasizes making and interpreting graphs, solving addition and subtraction story problems, and using efficient strategies for adding and subtracting 2-digit numbers. This unit also teaches representing these operations on a number line, which helps students visualize and better understand the concepts.

Unit 4 — Measurement

Shifts the focus to estimating, measuring, and comparing the lengths of objects using standard units like inches, feet, and yards. Students learn to select and use appropriate tools for measuring and to understand the importance of accuracy in measurement. They also practice measuring the length of an object using two different units, such as inches and then feet, which reinforces the concept of equivalent measurements in different units.

This quarter's curriculum not only enhances students' numerical and operational understanding but also integrates practical skills that are essential in everyday life.

🎨 Project-Based Learning

“Seed Stories: How Plants Trust Nature” — In this project, second grade students become young nature explorers as they investigate how seeds travel and how new plants grow in different places. They discover that plants "trust" animals, wind, and water to help move their seeds, creating natural relationships that support life. Through careful observations, simple experiments, model-building, reading, and storytelling, students learn that seed dispersal is part of a living system where plants and animals depend on each other.

Students connect their learning to real examples from Costa Rica, such as monkeys eating fruit and spreading seeds, birds dropping seeds as they fly, or the wind carrying light seeds across open spaces. This helps children see how nature works all around them and how every living thing plays a role.

Driving Question: How can we, as forestry engineers, study the ways plants trust animals to move their seeds and use this knowledge to care for our environment?

Science Focus: Life Sciences — Ecosystem Roles and Functions

  • Planning and conducting investigations to discover if plants need sunlight and water to grow
  • Developing models that mimic the role of animals in seed dispersal or plant pollination
  • Observing plants and animals to examine life diversity in various habitats

Social Studies Focus: My Community, Maps and the Natural World

Students explore the geographic and environmental relationships between pollinators and seed dispersal. They use maps and models to ask and answer questions about the locations of plants and pollinators and their connections. By distinguishing between human-made features and natural environments, students investigate how human activities impact pollinators and their habitats. They also examine how environmental changes in their community influence human actions. Through this project, students participate in solving a local issue related to pollinators and seed dispersal.

PBL Skills Focus: Critical Thinking, Complex Communication, and Collaboration

  • Critical Thinking: Explaining using important facts, making inferences, and drawing conclusions.
  • Complex Communication: Utilizing simple and descriptive language for clear articulation of topics, and adapting communication styles for different audiences.
  • Collaboration: Engaging in effective group planning, conflict resolution, staying on task, using academic language in discussions, and offering and seeking assistance.

Assessment

  • Interdependent Relationships in Ecosystems (Science): Assess students' understanding of plant needs (sunlight, water) through their investigation results. Evaluate their ability to develop and explain models that demonstrate animal roles in seed dispersal or pollination. Observe and assess their observational skills in comparing plant and animal diversity across different habitats.
  • My Community and the Natural Environment (Social Studies): Students demonstrate their understanding by using maps or models to explain the connections between pollinators, plants, and their locations. They distinguish between natural environments and human-made features, describe how human actions impact pollinators, and propose solutions to address local environmental challenges related to pollinators and seed dispersal.

Unidad: “Cambios Ambientales: Estudio del Impacto de los Cambios Ambientales en los Hábitats”

Análisis de Textos

Continuando con la exploración narrativa, nos enfocaremos en "Miravella y el dragón", un texto descriptivo. Los estudiantes analizarán las características de los personajes y del entorno del texto, desarrollando habilidades de comprensión y descripción.

Producción Escrita

En la producción escrita, nos centraremos en textos descriptivos, abordando el uso de la coma, el punto y los dos puntos en una lista. También se prestará atención a la concordancia entre el artículo, el sustantivo, el adjetivo y el verbo en una oración.

Producción Oral

En la producción oral, exploraremos el uso de la letra 'r' y 'rr', así como la correcta utilización de las letras 'que' y 'qui'. Los estudiantes practicarán la descripción oral de personajes y ambientes, fortaleciendo su capacidad de expresión verbal.

Actividades

  • Descripción oral de personajes y entornos
  • Creación de textos descriptivos en grupo
  • Prácticas de pronunciación de sonidos específicos

Habilidades

  • Análisis descriptivo y comprensión de textos
  • Desarrollo de habilidades de escritura descriptiva
  • Mejora en la pronunciación de sonidos específicos

Metas

  • Mejorar la capacidad de descripción oral y escrita
  • Reforzar las habilidades de análisis descriptivo

Proyecto

Creación de un mural descriptivo del entorno natural.

Evaluación

  • Evaluación continua de la participación en actividades
  • Evaluación de la capacidad de descripción oral y escrita
  • Evaluación de la mejora en la pronunciación
