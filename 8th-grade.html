📖 Language Arts

Resilience and Power: Analyzing Identity and Overcoming Adversity in The Breadwinner

In this integrated reading and writing unit, students will strengthen their reading comprehension, analytical thinking, and ability to express insights about literature. Through a focus on reading identity and comprehension strategies — such as summarizing, analyzing complex ideas, and examining power systems — students will learn to approach challenging texts with curiosity and confidence.

The core novel The Breadwinner by Deborah Ellis serves as the anchor text, offering students rich opportunities to analyze resilience, oppression, and the dynamics of power through Parvana’s experiences in Taliban-controlled Afghanistan.

Alongside this reading work, students will learn to craft literary essays that clearly communicate their interpretations of the text. They will develop skills in thesis writing, organizing ideas, selecting and explaining textual evidence, and refining their arguments through structured revision. By uniting deep reading with clear, purposeful writing, this unit supports students in building a nuanced understanding of literature and expressing their ideas with clarity and confidence.

🔢 Mathematics

Module 2: Functions and the Number System

In this module, students expand their understanding of numbers and relationships by exploring the real number system, powers and exponents, and the concept of functions. Students focus on how quantities change, how numbers are represented, and how inputs and outputs are connected. The module emphasizes structure, patterns, and multiple representations.

What Students Will Do

  • Classify and compare real numbers
  • Work with powers, exponents, and square roots
  • Use scientific notation to represent large and small numbers
  • Understand and define functions
  • Represent functions using tables, graphs, and equations
  • Interpret functions in real-world contexts

Topic 1: The Real Number System

Students classify numbers as rational or irrational and understand how square roots relate to the real number system. They compare values and explain relationships between different types of numbers.

Standards: 8.NS.1, 8.NS.2

Topic 2: Powers, Exponents and Scientific Notation

Students apply properties of exponents, work with powers of 10, and use scientific notation to represent very large or very small numbers. Emphasis is placed on reasoning, estimation, and real-world applications.

Standards: 8.EE.1, 8.EE.3, 8.EE.4

Topic 3: Introduction to Functions

Students learn what makes a relationship a function and how to represent functions using tables, graphs, equations, and verbal descriptions. They analyze inputs and outputs and interpret functions in context.

Standards: 8.F.1, 8.F.2, 8.F.3, 8.F.4, 8.F.5

🔬 Science

History of Earth

The performance expectations help students formulate answers to the questions: “How do people figure out that the Earth and life on Earth have changed over time?” and “How does the movement of tectonic plates impact the surface of Earth?” Four sub-ideas from the NRC Framework are addressed in these performance expectations: ESS1.C, ESS2.A, ESS2.B, and ESS2.C.

Students can examine geoscience data in order to understand the processes and events in Earth’s history. Important concepts in this topic are “Scale, Proportion, and Quantity” and “Stability and Change,” in relation to the different ways geologic processes operate over the long expanse of geologic time.

An important aspect of the history of Earth is that geologic events and conditions have affected the evolution of life, but different life forms have also played important roles in altering Earth’s systems. In the MS History of Earth performance expectations, students are expected to demonstrate proficiency in analyzing and interpreting data, and constructing explanations; and to use these practices to demonstrate understanding of the core ideas.

🌍 History

Colonialism in the Americas (15th – 18th Centuries)

During the second quarter, students will study the period of colonialism in the 15th to 18th centuries in the Americas. To do so, they will study a sampling of indigenous cultures of the continent to understand what life was like here before the arrival of Europeans, and then explore how the arrival of the Spanish, Portuguese, English, and French changed life.

We will look at colonial structures and systems and the impacts these had on the local populations and look at the global impact of the Columbian Exchange. Additionally, we will begin touching on emerging trends to lay down the foundations for later independence movements through the continent.

Students will continue to work with historical reading and thinking skills, including through examining primary and secondary documents, further developing comparison and contrast skills, and beginning to develop more analysis into their writing.

🇨🇷 Español

Rutas Literarias: Uvieta — Estudio integral que abarca tanto la narrativa (cuento) como la dramaturgia (teatro).

Metas de Aprendizaje

  • Desarrollar un análisis retórico profundo aplicado a discursos y crónicas.
  • Perfeccionar la oralidad mediante el debate formal y el uso de apoyos multimodales.
  • Dominar la sintaxis avanzada y el uso intencional del léxico para elevar el registro comunicativo.

Contenido

  • Literatura y Retórica: Análisis de Uvieta y estudio de discursos y crónicas.
  • Lengua: Sintaxis avanzada, conectores lógicos, control de la cohesión y adecuación del registro.
  • Léxico y Estilo: Vocabulario preciso y variaciones estilísticas intencionales.

Análisis de Textos

  • Investigación y desglose de artículos, ensayos breves, crónicas y reportajes.
  • Evaluación crítica de argumentos y estrategias retóricas básicas.

Producción Oral

  • Debate formal: Ejercicio de argumentación lógica y claridad.
  • Presentaciones temáticas: Exposiciones con apoyo multimodal.

Producción Escrita

  • Redacción de ensayos breves y análisis de investigación.
  • Aplicación de conectores lógicos y estructuras sintácticas complejas para asegurar la fluidez.

Actividades

  • Exposición formal sobre conceptos de retórica.
  • “Puente” analítico: Actividad de conexión entre discursos, crónicas y herramientas retóricas.
  • Análisis de fuentes de investigación (reportajes y artículos).

Oportunidades de Evaluación

  • Evaluación de la participación y desempeño en el debate formal.
  • Calificación de la exposición temática y el uso de recursos multimodales.
  • Revisión de ensayos y análisis retóricos escritos.

Conexión Interdisciplinaria

Vinculación con Estudios Sociales e Historia a través del análisis de discursos y crónicas que reflejan contextos sociopolíticos.
