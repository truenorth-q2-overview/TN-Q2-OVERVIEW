Toddler

“The Giants”

Welcome to the "Giants" group at Let's Play by True North. The Giants are our youngest students at the school — our Toddlers are turning 3 during the year, embarking on big and important milestones in their cognitive, social, and emotional development. This is a year filled with lots of play, exploration, and joyful growth as we begin this exciting journey together!

How Do We Learn Throughout the Year?

In our Toddlers program, we encourage student interactions and facilitate social and emotional learning throughout the day through play. Children guide the play processes and choose activities based on their current interests, fostering autonomy and self-expression.

We support these playful environments by offering experiences centered around foundational skills that promote cognitive, physical, and language development. Through intentional observation and engagement, we tailor activities to nurture problem-solving, creativity, and collaboration. Our approach ensures that children feel empowered and supported as they explore, grow, and build meaningful connections.

How and When Are Parents Informed About Growth?

  • Developmental Milestones Report Card (DMR): Based on the True North curriculum for early ages and developmental expectations, teachers identify and collect evidence of growth. This growth is observed in daily interactions, exploration time, and playful experiences. Sent home at the end of every quarter via e-mail.
  • Student Portfolio: Twice a year parents receive a digital portfolio with pictures and videos of each child reflecting their growth along overall standards.
  • Parent-Teacher Conferences: Concluding each semester, teachers will meet with all families.
  • Show My Learning: A presentation designed to connect school and family and showcase how children experience preschool life.

Awaken Your True North Program

Our Awaken Your True North program positions the development of essential skills for life and leadership (core skills) as the backbone of our educational model, transforming the school experience into an awakening of conscience and contributing to students' well-being and success as agents of change in society.

Our core skills are: self-care, appreciation, audacity, commitment, curiosity, empathy, and neutrality. Throughout the day our program enriches students' day with different practices.

Areas of Development

💜 Social & Emotional Learning

Social-emotional development serves as the foundation for much of a child's learning. We guide children to develop skills that foster strong relationships, enhance emotional awareness, and enable them to recognize, understand, and appropriately express their feelings.

Using the Conscious Discipline approach, we support toddlers in exploring their emotions, using their "Big Voice" to assert themselves and resolve social conflicts, and learning strategies to maintain composure.

🌱 Care of Self

During this school year, the Giants will develop and refine essential self-care skills, such as putting on their shoes, eating independently, and managing bathroom routines. Each child progresses at their own pace, and these daily tasks play a vital role in fostering their independence.

💬 Language Development

Giants are continuously learning and developing their language skills. Our program supports growth of their first language (Spanish, if applicable) while progressively immersing them in an English-speaking environment.

  • Support language expression and comprehension
  • Encourage understanding and repetition of English commands
  • Foster the ability to retell stories from books
  • Cultivate enjoyment of stories through questions and personal connections
  • Engage in playful exploration of sounds through rhymes and songs
  • Recognize their written name

🏃 Physical Development

  • Spatial Relationships: Children deepen their understanding of spatial concepts like up and down, in and out, and near and far.
  • Fine Motor: Children enhance the small, coordinated movements of their hands and fingers through grasping objects, manipulating tools, and precise activities. Outdoor play is key.
  • Gross Motor: Children strengthen their ability to control and coordinate large muscle groups through crawling, walking, running, and climbing.

🧠 Cognitive Development

Children explore and engage with the world around them, using imaginative play to express ideas and experiment with new concepts. They recall and connect personal experiences, showing curiosity through exploration and problem-solving during play.

Early math skills emerge as they recognize and match objects, identify shapes, grasp the concept of "one," and understand "more" in familiar contexts. They begin to mimic counting, say numbers aloud, and follow routine sequences.
