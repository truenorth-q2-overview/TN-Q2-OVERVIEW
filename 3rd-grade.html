Lower Elementary · Quarter II 2026

3rd Grade

📖 Language Arts

Reading Workshop

In this unit, students will learn to make careful, close observations of characters and then use their insights to craft theories and predictions. They will gain an understanding of the universal structures present in all stories, enabling them to achieve another goal for this quarter: thinking comparatively about characters in different books. This involves noting similarities and differences in these characters' struggles, motivations, reactions, and the lessons they learn.

Additionally, the focus will be on comprehension strategies to deepen their understanding and foster growth as readers. Students will work on understanding plot and setting, thinking critically about characters, and grasping themes and ideas in the books they read. Furthermore, they will focus on understanding vocabulary and figurative language in various student-selected books. As part of this work, the class read Because of Winn-Dixie by Kate DiCamillo, using the story to apply strategies for analyzing characters, themes, and figurative language.

Writing Workshop

The upcoming quarter promises an enchanting journey within the realms of the writing workshop. Students are set to explore the art of adapting fairy tales, an experience that is bound to captivate their imagination. This journey begins with the use of familiar fairy tales as springboards, allowing students to delve into the intricacies of fiction writing. Throughout this process, they will be guided in crafting scenes, using an omniscient narrator to orient readers, employing story structure to weave tension, and incorporating figurative language to evoke intricate moods. Additionally, we will intertwine social studies into the curriculum by focusing on Asian folktales and fairy tales.

🔢 Mathematics

In the second quarter of 3rd grade, Units 3 and 4 expand students' mathematical skills with a focus on arithmetic, measurement, time, and fractions.

Unit 3 — Addition & Subtraction within 100

Strengthens students' abilities in addition and subtraction within 100, where they engage in making graphs, solving story problems, and using efficient strategies for computation. They learn to represent addition and subtraction on a number line, enhancing their understanding of these operations.

Unit 4 — Time, Measurement & Fractions

Introduces students to the concepts of time and measurement. They learn to tell and calculate elapsed time, an essential skill for understanding the passage of time in practical scenarios. The unit also covers the measurement of mass and volume, where students solve problems that require them to measure and compare different quantities. Additionally, Unit 4 delves into the world of fractions, teaching students to model and compare fractions in various ways. This helps in developing a solid foundation in fraction concepts, preparing them for more advanced mathematical operations in the future.

Together, these units provide a comprehensive and challenging mathematical experience, combining core arithmetic skills with practical applications and new mathematical concepts.

🎨 Project-Based Learning

“Guardians of our Habitats” — Students will spend the quarter thinking, investigating, and creating like a real scientist. Working in a small team, students will choose a local habitat — such as a forest, wetland, or urban park — and dig deep into how the plants and animals that live there depend on each other to survive.

The project builds toward a real public product: a Habitat Action Plan that students will present at the Student Led Conference. Their plan will identify a threat to their chosen habitat and propose a science-based solution — just like environmental scientists do.

Driving Question: How can we, as ecosystem scientists, warn our community about a local habitat in danger — and propose a real solution?

Science Focus: Inheritance and Variation of Traits — Life Cycles and Traits

Students will study habitats and construct arguments on group survival behaviors in animals.

Social Studies Focus: Natural Heritage

Students will examine natural heritage in the world and Costa Rica, exploring the interaction between human activities and the environment. They will connect causes and effects of environmental changes, recognize global patterns, and compare citizen responsibilities in local and world communities.

PBL Skills Focus

  • Collaboration: Engaging in group planning, conflict resolution, and staying on task.
  • Complex communication: Combine simple and descriptive vocabulary to explain the topics. Speak appropriately for various situations and audiences.
  • Critical thinking: Summarize topics and concepts, supporting important details with evidence. Make inferences and draw simple conclusions.

Assessment

  • Organism Profile Card (Science): Students conduct investigations and analyze data in order to write arguments about animals and their habitats.
  • Natural heritage in Central America and other continents (Social Studies): Students will identify and explain the causes and effects of environmental changes, using examples from history, current events, or their own lives. They will describe how human activities impact the environment and how natural environments shape human actions in specific world communities. Students will compare the rights and responsibilities of citizens in local and world communities, emphasizing their role in protecting the environment.

🇨🇷 Español

Unidad: “Enfoque en País: Omán — Geografía y Recursos Naturales”

Análisis de Textos

En este segundo bimestre, nos adentraremos en el texto descriptivo "El Principito". Los estudiantes se centrarán en el uso del punto, la coma, los dos puntos y los signos de admiración e interrogación, así como en los tiempos verbales y la concordancia entre sujeto, predicado y tiempos verbales.

Producción Escrita

La producción escrita se enfocará en usos específicos de las letras 'c' y 's', y se explorará el acento y la sílaba tónica. Los estudiantes aplicarán estos conocimientos en la descripción detallada de las características de los personajes y los ambientes en los que se desarrolla el texto.

Producción Oral

Los estudiantes profundizarán en la biografía y la lucha por la independencia, compartiendo sus hallazgos de manera coherente y fluida en la exposición oral.

Actividades

  • Proyectos de elaboración de mapas resaltando los recursos naturales
  • Estudios comparativos sobre cómo diferentes regiones utilizan sus recursos naturales
  • Discusiones en el aula sobre el impacto del uso de recursos en entornos locales y globales

Habilidades

  • Desarrollo de habilidades descriptivas en la escritura
  • Aplicación de conocimientos geográficos en la producción escrita
  • Práctica de la expresión oral y presentación de información de manera coherente

Metas

  • Mejorar la escritura descriptiva y el análisis geográfico
  • Fomentar la conciencia ambiental y la comprensión global

Proyecto

Construcción de un modelo representativo de la geografía y recursos naturales de una región específica.

Evaluación

  • Evaluación continua de la participación en actividades
  • Evaluación de la mejora en la escritura descriptiva
  • Evaluación de la presentación oral y comprensión ambiental
